Dear Editors,

I am submitting for your consideration the manuscript "Tipping the Climate Equilibrium: Tensor-Based Game Theory for Identifying Critical Coalitions in Climate Policy Negotiations" for publication in Environmental and Resource Economics.

The paper introduces a third-order tensor model of strategic interaction in multilateral climate negotiations and uses it to identify small coalitions of non-G20 nations capable of shifting the system toward a high-cooperation equilibrium. The central theoretical contribution is a formal proof that, for the real-data influence tensor, the first-order Z-eigenvalue shift under coalition activation equals a weighted combination of internal alignment and outward influence spread — providing exact algebraic grounding for the composite tipping score metric. On the empirical side, a REINFORCE policy gradient agent with Gumbel top-K sampling discovers high-scoring coalitions from a space of 175 countries and 263 treaties, achieving a 14.7% improvement over random search and identifying Uruguay, Tunisia, Greece, and Algeria as keystone coalition anchors. A key finding is that cross-cluster geographic diversity spanning at least six of eight treaty-participation communities is a necessary condition for top-scoring coalitions — operationalising the intuition that the most influential alliances are the least obvious.

I believe this manuscript fits Environmental and Resource Economics because it combines formal economic theory with a novel computational method and a large-scale real-data application directly relevant to international environmental policy. The framework offers applied value for coalition advisors and climate negotiators in addition to its methodological contributions, and connects to the journal's established interest in coalition formation and international environmental agreements.

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under review at any other journal. A preprint has been posted to arXiv (econ.GN / cs.GT) to establish priority; this is disclosed in accordance with the journal's preprint policy. All data and code will be made publicly available upon acceptance.
